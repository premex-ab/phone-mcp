# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and respectful environment for all members of our community. This Code of Conduct applies to all spaces managed by the Android MCP Server project, including GitHub repositories, discussions, issues, pull requests, and any official project communications.

We believe that a diverse and inclusive community makes our project stronger and more innovative. We are dedicated to creating a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, political belief, race, religion, sexual identity and orientation, or socioeconomic status.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contribute to creating a positive environment include:

- **Be Respectful**: Treat all members with courtesy and respect, even when you disagree
- **Be Inclusive**: Welcome diverse perspectives and backgrounds; make everyone feel valued
- **Be Professional**: Keep discussions focused on the project and maintain a professional tone
- **Be Constructive**: Provide helpful feedback that is specific, actionable, and aimed at improvement
- **Be Patient**: Remember that community members have different skills, experience levels, and backgrounds
- **Be Accountable**: Take responsibility for your words and actions; apologize and learn when you make mistakes
- **Ask Questions**: If you don't understand something, ask politely rather than making assumptions
- **Share Knowledge**: Help others learn and grow; mentor newcomers to the project
- **Celebrate Success**: Acknowledge and appreciate contributions from community members

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not tolerated within our community:

- **Harassment**: Any form of harassment, including but not limited to, offensive comments related to gender, gender identity and expression, sexual orientation, disability, mental illness, neuroatypicality, physical appearance, body size, age, race, ethnicity, or religion
- **Discrimination**: Exclusion or discrimination based on protected characteristics or personal identity
- **Sexual Harassment**: Sexual comments, jokes, or advances of any kind, unwelcome sexual attention
- **Intimidation or Threats**: Threats of violence or intimidating behavior toward any individual
- **Trolling**: Deliberately disruptive comments or behavior designed to provoke or upset others
- **Spam**: Repeated unwanted messages, self-promotion, or off-topic content
- **Doxxing**: Sharing private information (addresses, phone numbers, etc.) without consent
- **Brigading**: Organizing groups to attack, harass, or vote down members of the community
- **Abuse of Reporting**: Making false reports or misusing the reporting system to target individuals

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All project repositories, wikis, and code review platforms
- Issue trackers and pull request discussions
- Project mailing lists and communication channels
- Official project social media accounts
- In-person events organized by the project
- Any official project communications

The Code of Conduct also applies when someone is representing the project or its community in external spaces (e.g., conferences, public forums, social media).

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

If you witness or experience behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it by:

1. **Direct Communication**: Try to resolve minor issues directly with the person involved, if you feel safe doing so
2. **Contact Maintainers**: Email the project maintainers with details of the incident
3. **GitHub Reporting**: Use GitHub's built-in reporting tools for comments and content
4. **Emergency**: For threats of violence or urgent safety concerns, contact local authorities immediately

When reporting, please include:
- Description of the incident
- When and where it occurred
- Who was involved
- Any relevant context or evidence
- How the incident affected you or others

**Reports will be kept confidential.** All reports will be reviewed and investigated appropriately.

### Consequences

The project team is committed to enforcing this Code of Conduct fairly and consistently. Depending on the severity of the violation, consequences may include:

1. **Warning**: A private or public notice requesting the person to cease the behavior
2. **Temporary Suspension**: Temporary removal from community spaces (hours to weeks)
3. **Permanent Ban**: Permanent removal from the project community

The project team will:
- Assess the situation objectively
- Consider the context and severity of the violation
- Give the person opportunity to respond
- Take appropriate action to protect the community
- Document the incident and resolution

Appeals of enforcement actions may be made to the project maintainers with additional context or information.

## Reporting Procedures

### For Community Members

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please:

1. **Collect Information**: Document the incident (date, time, description, participants)
2. **Report to Maintainers**: Send a detailed report via email or GitHub issue to the project maintainers
3. **Follow Up**: Check in if you haven't received acknowledgment within 48 hours
4. **Support Others**: Offer support to anyone affected by the incident

### For Maintainers

When investigating reports, maintainers will:

1. **Acknowledge**: Send acknowledgment to the reporter within 24 hours
2. **Investigate**: Gather information from all parties involved
3. **Decide**: Determine appropriate action based on the facts and this Code of Conduct
4. **Communicate**: Inform the reporter and involved parties of the decision
5. **Follow Up**: Check in with all parties to ensure the situation is resolved

**Confidentiality**: Maintain confidentiality of reporters and involved parties, except where necessary for safety.

## Special Cases

### Self-Defense

If someone is defending themselves from harassment or abuse, the use of equivalent force is permitted and will not be considered a violation of this Code of Conduct.

### Accidental Offense

If you unintentionally violate this Code of Conduct, we encourage you to:
- Listen to feedback without being defensive
- Apologize sincerely
- Make concrete efforts to change your behavior
- Ask for guidance if you're unsure

Most community members are willing to accept genuine apologies and growth.

## Resources & Support

### For Targets of Harassment

- Reach out to project maintainers for support
- Document the incidents for your own records
- Consider seeking support from trusted friends or mentors
- Don't hesitate to step back from the project if you need to

### For Those Who Have Acted Wrongly

- Listen to the feedback you've received
- Take responsibility for your actions
- Make meaningful changes to your behavior
- Seek to repair any harm caused
- Consider reaching out to the person(s) you affected, if appropriate

### Community Support Resources

- Contributor resources (to be provided by maintainers)
- Mental health and support services
- Conflict resolution guidance
- Educational materials on inclusive behavior
